The Future of Cryptography

Today
Classical Cryptography Highly secure against modern computers. Relies on the extreme difficulty of factoring massive prime numbers (RSA).
Quantum
The Quantum Threat Future quantum computers could run Shor's algorithm, instantly breaking today's RSA and ECC public key cryptography.
Post-Quantum
The Solution (PQC) New mathematical algorithms (like lattice-based cryptography) designed to resist both classical and quantum computing attacks.
Zero-Knowledge Future Digital Identity
Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKP) A cryptographic method that allows you to prove you know a secret (like being over 18) without ever revealing the actual data.
